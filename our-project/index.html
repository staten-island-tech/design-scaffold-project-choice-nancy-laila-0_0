Cuisine Finder

Hawaii

1. Pineapple Fried Rice

This Hawaiian pineapple fried rice makes the perfect summer time meal when pineapples are in season! This dish is full of sweet, juicy pineapple chunks, colorful vegetables, and leftover cooked rice.

2. Poke

The traditional Hawaiian poke consists of fish that has been gutted, skinned, and deboned. It is served with traditional condiments such as Hawaiian sea salt, candlenut, seaweed, and limu.

3. Poi

In Hawaii, where poi is a staple of local cuisine, taro root is used almost exclusively. The peeled roots are cooked, pounded, mixed with water to the desired consistency, and strained to remove fibres.

4. Haupia

Haupia is a traditional coconut milk-based Hawaiian dessert often found at luaus and other local gatherings. A popular variation is haupia pie where it is accompanied with either chocolate or purple sweet potato.

Fiji

1. Nama

Indigenous to Fiji, nama is a type of edible seaweed mainly harvested in pristine, shallow waters. Because of its unique shape, formed of tiny, crunchy spheres, it is also known as sea grapes.

2. Kokoda

Fiji Kokoda, a raw fish salad, is considered to be the national dish and is found all over the islands. It is served with coconut milk in order to balance out the lime juice and served with many different toppings.

3. Fiji Lovo

The term 'lovo' also refers to the underground oven that is used to cook the feast. This traditional technique involves digging a pit into the ground and placing hot coals inside, where food is then cooked.

4. Babakau

Also known as Fijian pancake or donut, babakau is a typical Fijian breakfast meal that is usually eaten with sugar, jam, butter and some lemon or lime wedges on the side. It is a light and airy deep-fried dough.

Samoa

1. Sapa Sui

Sapa sui consists of meat and quickly seared vegetables like as carrots, bok choy and red peppers that are sautéed in a sauce made of soy sauce, garlic and ginger.

2. Masi Somoa

Masi Samoa are buttery, coconut shortbread from Samoa. The key ingredient is coconut milk, it gives the cookies a nice subtle coconut flavor.

3. Pani Popo

Pani Popo is a Samoan sweet roll baked in a delicious coconut sauce. They are fluffy yeast rolls soaked in a sweet coconut syrup mixture and baked until golden brown.

4. Panikeke

Panikeke are Samoan version of pancakes, characterized by their round shape. They consist of flour, sugar, eggs, and milk, and are accompanied by fruit or jams.

Palau

1. Halo-Halo

Halo-Halo is an extremely popular Paluan dessert. It's a mixture of ice cream, shaved ice, and condesned milk. It's layers of goodness, and a popular summer dessert.

2. Tama

Tama is a Palauan specialty made with eggs, milk, flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. The batter is flavored with vanilla, then fried in hot oil.

3. Ulkoy

Ulkoy are deep-fried Palauan shrimp and squash fritters with a crunchy texture. In Palau, they are a national favorite along with being a delicious treat for the tourists.

4. Udon

Udon is a Palauan dish made with a soy sauce-based broth and spaghetti, since it is easier to acquire it than the authentic udon noodles.
